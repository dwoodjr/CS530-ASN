About Me

Hi! I’m Darren Woodland Jr. (he/him), an Afrofuturist and experimental media artist exploring the intersections of technology and culture within interactive sonic fictions.

I’m currently pursuing a Ph.D. in Digital Media at Drexel University. I have a Master’s of Art and Design in Experimental Media Arts from North Carolina State University and a double Bachelor's in Media Arts and Art Studio from the University of South Carolina.

I’ve worked as a technical artist, sound implementer, and researcher and my work (or projects I have contributed to) have been showcased at Ars Electronica, SXSW, SIGGRAPH, and other venues and conferences in the US and abroad. This portfolio showcases some of my projects, where I combine creativity and technology to bring ideas to life.

Projects

Meal Planner App

We want to create a recipe finder and meal planner application that would allow for users to search up recipes based on ingredients, cooking time, or other restrictions. Users would be able to generate a grocery shopping list based on a selected recipe.

Harmonic Threads

An interdisciplinary research project around a designer-dancer workshop that utilized real-time tools and technologies to mediate an Afrofuturist sonic narrative through dance. This work was accepted and showcased at Ars Electronica 2023.

Plumes and Pixels

A joint group research project that explored the uses of novel techniques in Gaussian Splatting for natural sciences and archival purposes. The project simultaneously explored "SciArt" explorations; scientific research and data used as artistic inspiration by artists and designers. This project, in conjunction with the Academy of Natural Sciences was turned into a paper, submitted, and accepted to SIGGRAPH Asia 2024. Part of my contributions are showcased above.
